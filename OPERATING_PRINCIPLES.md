# BOSS Operating Principles

## Purpose

These principles govern company decisions across product, engineering, design,
sales, support, operations, AI agents, automations, and releases.

1. **Customer outcomes over features.** Work begins with a measurable business
   outcome.
2. **Execution before intelligence.** Advice creates value only when it leads
   to governed action or a better decision.
3. **Trust before automation.** BOSS earns broader authority through verified
   execution.
4. **Evidence before opinion.** Recommendations, dashboards, learning, and
   claims reference evidence.
5. **One business model.** Industries specialize the CBM; they do not replace
   it.
6. **One runtime.** New business execution uses UCR and one evidence/governance
   lifecycle.
7. **Learn through work.** Progressive discovery replaces prolonged software
   configuration.
8. **Progressive value.** Every meaningful interaction should improve clarity,
   action, trust, or a business outcome.
9. **Explain every decision.** State what happened, why, evidence, risk,
   expected outcome, and confidence.
10. **Respect the owner's time.** Save time, reduce effort or mistakes, increase
    value, or reduce stress.
11. **Keep complexity invisible.** Customers receive clear priorities and
    actions, not platform internals.
12. **Disclose progressively.** Show the controls and information appropriate
    to the customer's current need and maturity.
13. **Amplify human leadership.** Owners define vision, boundaries, and
    approvals.
14. **Improve continuously.** Verified outcomes improve future decisions,
    execution, and business operations.
15. **Strengthen the platform.** New verticals reuse canonical state,
    execution, evidence, and governance.
16. **Protect Business Memory.** It remains a canonical evidence-backed graph
    view, never fragmented private state.
17. **Verify outcomes.** Technical completion alone is not business success.
18. **Small business first.** The default experience must work for businesses
    with 1, 5, or 20 employees.
19. **Every screen leads to action.** Explain what happened, why it matters,
    what comes next, and what BOSS can do.
20. **Earn the right to grow.** Request more data, permissions, integrations,
    or automation only after delivering value.

## Decision Filter

### Customer Value

- What measurable business outcome improves?
- Does this shorten time to value or reduce owner effort?

### Execution

- Which Business Operating Loop stage improves?
- Does execution use UCR?
- Is the outcome measurable and replayable?

### Trust

- Is the recommendation explainable and evidence-backed?
- Is action governed, reversible where applicable, and owner-controlled?

### Architecture

- Does it map to the Canonical Business Model?
- Does it strengthen canonical Business Graph/Memory state?
- Does it avoid duplicate state, orchestration, and runtime paths?

A failed answer sends the work back to design.

## Subtract Before You Add

Before adding an agent, service, table, workflow, runtime, dashboard, or
integration, answer:

1. Can an existing capability be extended?
2. Can the CBM and Business Graph already represent it?
3. Can UCR already execute it?
4. Can existing decision capabilities reason about it?
5. Can an Industry Pack specialize it?

New platform primitives require five evidence-backed “no” answers, ARB review,
and an ADR.
