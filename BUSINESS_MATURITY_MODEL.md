# BOSS Business Maturity Model

## Purpose

The Business Maturity Model (BMM) is the customer roadmap. It measures how
reliably a business observes, decides, executes, verifies, and learns relative
to its own goals. It is distinct from the BOSS Platform Maturity Model, which
measures engineering capability.

Maturity is evidence-based. BOSS may not promote a business because a feature
was enabled, an integration was connected, or a task was completed.

## Level 0: Chaos

The business is owner-dependent, reactive, and poorly documented.

**BOSS mission:** establish visibility and trust.

Exit evidence:

- Canonical business identity and intent established.
- Initial Business Graph and Memory view available.
- Business health baseline is evidence-backed.
- First approved execution and outcome verification complete.
- Owner can see what happened, why, and what comes next.

## Level 1: Organized

Roles, customers, goals, priorities, operating cadence, and essential processes
are visible and versioned.

**BOSS mission:** create operational clarity.

Exit evidence:

- Ownership and responsibilities resolve through the Business Graph.
- Critical processes have approved operating definitions.
- Priorities and KPIs have owners and current evidence.
- Executive briefing is generated from canonical state.
- Exceptions and missing information are visible.

## Level 2: Automated

Repeatable, policy-approved work executes consistently with verification.

**BOSS mission:** reduce manual effort without reducing control.

Exit evidence:

- Eligible routine executions use UCR.
- Approval policy and progressive automation level are explicit.
- Failures, evidence, and outcomes are observable.
- Manual effort and execution reliability are measured.
- Automation can be paused, explained, and governed.

## Level 3: Optimized

The business proactively identifies constraints, risks, and opportunities and
adapts approved plans from verified outcomes.

**BOSS mission:** improve operating performance.

Exit evidence:

- Opportunities are ranked by impact, urgency, confidence, risk, and goals.
- Decisions and plans link to evidence.
- Outcome trends demonstrate measurable improvement.
- Forecasts and recommendations declare uncertainty.
- Learning changes future decisions through versioned updates.

## Level 4: Executive

Cross-department decisions use shared business context, evidence, and outcome
governance.

**BOSS mission:** coordinate the business as one system.

Exit evidence:

- Executive decisions record consulted domains and tradeoffs.
- Department goals and dependencies align to business outcomes.
- Business health, risk, capital, people, and execution are reviewed together.
- The owner receives prioritized decisions rather than disconnected reports.
- Decision quality and execution impact are measurable.

## Level 5: Autonomous

The owner defines goals, boundaries, risk, and approval policy while BOSS
continuously coordinates eligible execution and verified adaptation.

**BOSS mission:** operate within earned trust.

Exit evidence:

- Goal-to-outcome loops run through certified UCR and governance.
- Autonomous scope is explicit, revocable, and policy-limited.
- Every execution is evidence-backed, observable, measurable, and replayable.
- Verified learning adjusts plans without bypassing approvals.
- The owner retains control of outcomes and operating boundaries.

## Evolution Path

```text
Chaos -> Organized -> Automated -> Optimized -> Executive -> Autonomous
```

Businesses may mature at different rates by department or capability. BOSS
reports both overall and domain maturity, records the evidence supporting each
assessment, and may lower a level when evidence becomes stale or controls fail.

## Capability Ladder

Every customer capability contributes to:

1. Observe.
2. Understand.
3. Decide.
4. Plan.
5. Execute.
6. Learn.

Verification is mandatory between Execute and Learn in the Business Operating
Loop.

## Business IQ

Business IQ is a future evidence-backed maturity index, not a generic quality
score. Candidate dimensions are visibility, process maturity, automation
adoption, execution reliability, decision quality, learning effectiveness, and
growth readiness.

No Business IQ score may ship until formulas, evidence references, confidence,
freshness, tenant isolation, and historical reproducibility are certified.
