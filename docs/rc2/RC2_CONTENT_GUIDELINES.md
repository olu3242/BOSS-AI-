# RC2 Content Guidelines

**Tone, Messaging Rules, and Outcome-First Writing for BOSS**

---

## Voice

BOSS speaks like a confident, calm business advisor — not a startup. Not a chatbot. Not a salesperson.

| Do | Don't |
|---|---|
| "BOSS gives you a clear picture of your numbers every day." | "Our AI-powered analytics dashboard provides real-time KPI visualization." |
| "You wake up. BOSS has already worked 5 hours." | "Leverage autonomous AI agents to automate overnight workflows." |
| "Invoice #3021 paid — Johnson & Co. · $3,400" | "Payment received. Transaction complete." |
| "3 new enquiries came in overnight. BOSS replied to all three." | "AI has processed 3 incoming leads." |

---

## Outcome-First Rule

Every feature must be written as an outcome the owner experiences, not a capability the software has.

**Formula:** `[Who benefits] [what changes] [measurable result]`

✓ "You spent 3 fewer hours on admin this week."  
✓ "BOSS got your no-show rate down 74% in the first month."  
✗ "Our scheduling automation reduces appointment non-attendance via reminder workflows."

---

## Numbers

- Use realistic, directional numbers that reflect real product capability
- Avoid made-up precision (don't say "97.3% reduction" — say "nearly eliminated")
- When numbers are estimates, mark them honestly (e.g., "avg. cash flow improvement")
- Stats in UI mocks should feel plausible for a real SMB: revenue $50K–$200K/mo, 10–50 staff

---

## Customer Language

Write from the owner's perspective, not the platform's.

Use: "you", "your business", "your customers", "your team"  
Avoid: "the user", "the operator", "our platform", "our AI"

---

## Industry Naming

Use the name the owner uses for their own industry:

| Say | Don't say |
|---|---|
| "Dental practices" | "Healthcare SMBs" |
| "Law firms" | "Legal services providers" |
| "Plumbing & HVAC" | "Field service operators" |
| "Gyms & fitness studios" | "Health & wellness SMEs" |

---

## What BOSS Is

Write about BOSS as:
- Your AI team
- Your business operating system
- The thing that runs in the background so you don't have to

Never write about BOSS as:
- A chatbot
- An automation tool
- A no-code platform
- An AI model or LLM

---

## Empty States

Every empty state must:
1. Acknowledge the state plainly ("No tasks yet")
2. Explain what will appear ("Your AI workforce activity will show here")
3. Give one clear next action ("Activate your first AI team member →")

---

## Error Messages

Every error must:
1. Say what happened in plain language
2. Say whether the user or the system caused it
3. Give one clear next step or support path

---

## CTA Copy Principles

- Primary CTA: specific action + implied benefit ("Get my free Business Health Report")
- Secondary CTA: lower commitment ("Book a live demo instead")
- Never use generic "Learn more", "Get started", "Sign up"
- Note time/effort where it removes friction ("Takes 10 minutes to set up. No tech skills needed.")
