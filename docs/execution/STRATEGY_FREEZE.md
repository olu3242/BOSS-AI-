# BOSS Strategy Freeze

## Decision

The foundational strategy is frozen as of 2026-06-28.

The frozen set is:

- Operating Principles.
- Execution Constitution.
- Product Operating Model.
- Canonical Business Model.
- Business Operating Loop.
- Business Maturity Model.
- Customer Lifecycle Framework.
- Master Program Plan and Production Increment map.

These documents define company decisions, product intent, business semantics,
continuous operation, customer evolution, customer journey, and delivery
sequence without overlapping ownership.

## What Comes Next

Future artifacts are implementation specifications and certifications:

1. Business Memory specification.
2. Business Signal protocol.
3. Remaining UCR resilience, scheduling, observability, and certification
   specifications.
4. Decision/Executive Council protocol.
5. Business Operating Loop state-machine specification.
6. Trust, approval, explainability, audit, and privacy specification.
7. Industry Pack extension specification.

Each specification defines contracts, schemas, APIs, lifecycle, events,
security, tenant isolation, evidence, observability, performance, migration,
and certification criteria.

A monolithic BOSS Execution Specification is not an approved artifact. Runtime,
Business Memory, approval, verification, replay, and decision contracts remain
separate bounded specifications so each can be implemented and certified
without reopening the platform architecture.

## Change Control

New foundational strategy requires:

- A demonstrated gap not covered by the frozen set.
- “Subtract Before You Add” evidence.
- ARB approval.
- An ADR identifying superseded or amended authority.
- Impact analysis for roadmaps, public contracts, and certifications.

New prompts do not alter the frozen strategy by themselves.
