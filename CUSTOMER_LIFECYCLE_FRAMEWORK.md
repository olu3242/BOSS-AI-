# BOSS Customer Lifecycle Framework

## Purpose

The Customer Lifecycle Framework (CLF) is the customer journey blueprint for
Product, Success, Marketing, Support, and Engineering. It complements the
Business Maturity Model:

- CLF describes the customer's relationship with BOSS.
- BMM describes the business's operating maturity.
- BOL describes continuous business operation.

Progress is evidence-based and may differ by organization or department.

## 1. Discover

**Customer question:** Can BOSS help a business like mine?

**Objective:** earn informed curiosity.

Evidence of success:

- The customer recognizes their business problem and intended outcome.
- Examples, assessment, and value claims are relevant and supportable.
- No fabricated health, ROI, or industry claims appear.

## 2. Trust

**Customer question:** Can I trust BOSS with my business?

**Objective:** establish transparency before requesting access.

Evidence of success:

- Data ownership, privacy, permissions, approvals, and explainability are clear.
- The first permission is voluntary and tied to visible value.

## 3. First Value

**Customer question:** Did I receive something useful immediately?

**Objective:** produce measurable value within five minutes.

Evidence of success:

- Canonical business context is established.
- One evidence-backed priority or opportunity is visible.
- One governed execution completes and its outcome is verified.

Executive briefings and health baselines count only when generated from
persisted canonical state.

## 4. Learn

**Customer question:** Is BOSS understanding how my business operates?

**Objective:** deepen canonical knowledge through work.

Evidence of success:

- Progressive discovery reduces missing information.
- Business Graph/Memory versions reflect verified facts and preferences.
- The customer can correct assumptions and see provenance.

## 5. Execute

**Customer question:** Is BOSS saving me time and effort?

**Objective:** move governed work from advice to action.

Evidence of success:

- Approved work executes through UCR.
- Evidence, telemetry, failures, and outcomes are visible.
- Routine owner effort decreases without loss of control.

## 6. Optimize

**Customer question:** Is BOSS helping me improve?

**Objective:** identify and act on opportunities before problems compound.

Evidence of success:

- Trends, opportunities, decisions, and plans reference current evidence.
- Verified outcomes improve future prioritization.
- Recommendations state uncertainty and business impact.

## 7. Scale

**Customer question:** Can the business grow without becoming chaotic?

**Objective:** preserve operating clarity through growth.

Evidence of success:

- New people, locations, and delegation use shared canonical models.
- Playbooks and policies remain consistent and versioned.
- Operational complexity grows slower than business capacity.

## 8. Evolve

**Customer question:** Is the business becoming less dependent on daily owner
coordination?

**Objective:** establish a governed self-improving operating model.

Evidence of success:

- Verified learning evolves blueprints, policies, plans, and priorities.
- Automation authority expands only through earned trust.
- The owner retains vision, boundaries, and revocation control.

## Customer Flywheel

```text
Discover -> Trust -> First Value -> Learn -> Execute -> Optimize -> Scale
-> Evolve -> Stronger Trust and Greater Value
```

## Product Adaptation

The product emphasizes the next lifecycle outcome rather than exposing every
module:

- New customers see first value, one priority, and one recommended execution.
- Growing customers see opportunities, delegation, and automation maturity.
- Mature customers see strategy, forecasting, multi-location performance, and
  continuous optimization.

Progressive disclosure must not hide permissions, risk, evidence, or controls.

## Success Engineering

A future Success capability may monitor first execution, briefing usefulness,
memory completeness, recommendation engagement, automation adoption, maturity,
and verified business value. It coaches customer progress; it does not execute
business work or create a second business state.

The Success capability is not implemented. It requires a separate CBM mapping,
privacy and tenant model, evidence-backed metrics, UCR integration where it
acts, and independent certification.
